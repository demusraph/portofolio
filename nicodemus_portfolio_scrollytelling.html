01 · 2025
RayLo — IoT Brake Safety Device
An aftermarket IoT device that prevents motorcycle brake failure by monitoring disc temperatures in real-time and alerting riders before brake fade occurs.
IoTProduct DesignUX ResearchDesign Thinking
OVERVIEW
Designed end-to-end as part of a 5-member Product Design team. RayLo addresses a critical safety gap: 64% of surveyed riders prioritised rapid heat danger alerts. Using 5W+1H and consumer surveys (25+ respondents), the team developed a plug-and-play, cognitively accessible solution for daily riders.
KEY OUTCOMES
  • 64% of respondents identified as target users in market research
  • Plug-and-play design — zero technical installation required
  • Addresses "silent failure" gap in existing motorcycle safety
  • Applied design-thinking across full ideation-to-prototype cycle

Quality Improvement — Lussie Juice
DMAIC-driven quality improvement initiative for a local SME, increasing the production sigma level from 1.67σ to 2.33σ through root cause analysis and new SOPs.
Six SigmaDMAICFMEALean Ops

03 · 2025
Bangladesh Disaster Relief Simulation
A discrete-event simulation model built in Simio to optimise the multi-modal distribution of 80,000 tons of humanitarian aid to 2 million residents within 40 days.
SimioSimulationLogisticsOptimisation
OVERVIEW
System Modelling project simulating disaster relief logistics in Bangladesh. Integrated helicopters, planes, ships, and trucks into a multi-modal strategy. Model validated through Face Validity and experimental data to reflect real-world constraints, balancing a $93.6M operational budget.
KEY OUTCOMES
  • 100% delivery of 80,000 tons to 2M residents in 40 days
  • Multi-modal strategy covering 4 transport types
  • Total cost optimised at ~$93.6M operational budget
  • Model validated via Face Validity & experimental testing
